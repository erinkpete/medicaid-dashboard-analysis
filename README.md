## Project Overview

**Question:** Which states receive the most Medicaid prescription support, and which drugs are driving reimbursement costs?

In 2021, State Medicaid programs collectively reimbursed over 1.46 billion prescriptions across the United States. This project explores where that support is concentrated, which medications are most in demand, and where disparities in access may exist.

**Intended Audience:** Pharmaceutical companies, Medicaid policymakers, and public health researchers interested in prescription access and drug cost trends.

---

## Key Findings

- **California and New York** led the nation in total Medicaid prescription reimbursements
- **Wyoming and South Dakota** had the lowest reimbursement totals, suggesting potential disparities in access or demand
- **Methylphenidate** (commonly used for ADHD) and **Gabapentin** (used for nerve pain and seizures) were the two most frequently reimbursed medications nationwide
- Significant variation exists across states, pointing to areas where increased Medicaid support and further research may be needed
